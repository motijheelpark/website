Our Story

Born from the
Pearl Lake of Bengal

Motijheel — the Pearl Lake — was once a jewel in the crown of Nawab Siraj ud-Daulah's Murshidabad. It was here, surrounded by the grandeur of a vanished empire, that a dream took root: to create a gathering place worthy of this sacred ground.

Mahfil-e-Motijheel was built not merely as a resort, but as a living tribute to Murshidabad's cultural legacy — a place where the warmth of Bengali hospitality meets the grandeur of Nawabi architecture, where guests don't just visit, they belong.

Today, our resort stands at the crossroads of three historic municipalities — Berhampore, Jiaganj-Azimganj, and Murshidabad — welcoming travellers who seek something deeper than a hotel stay: a genuine encounter with history, nature, and the unhurried grace of the Bhagirathi.

What Defines Us

Three Centuries of
Heritage in Every Corner

Murshidabad carries the weight of empires — and Mahfil-e-Motijheel carries that legacy forward through three founding convictions.

01

Place & History

Our address is not accidental. Motijheel was the recreational lake of the last independent Nawab of Bengal. When you walk our grounds, you walk where history was made. Every garden path, every river-facing corridor is a conversation with the past.

02

Warmth & Welcome

Bengali hospitality is not a policy — it is a philosophy. At Mahfil-e-Motijheel, every meal is made like it's for family, every greeting carries genuine warmth. Our team is rooted in the same community that built this land over generations.

03

Rest & Renewal

In the age of constant motion, we are a pause. River breezes carry the scent of mustard fields. Mornings begin slowly, over chai and birdsong. Our resort was designed to give you back the time and stillness that modern life takes away.

Our Vision

Heritage Kept
Beautifully Alive

We believe that the best way to honour a historical place is to make it breathe again — not as a museum, but as a living, welcoming space where culture is felt and shared every day.

"A mahfil is a gathering — of people, of stories, of music, of memory. We wanted this resort to be exactly that."

Every design choice, from the arched corridors to the lantern-lit gardens, is inspired by Nawabi Murshidabad's architectural vocabulary. Our rooms carry names drawn from the cultural life of the Nawabs — poetry, music, craftsmanship, river.
